For example, If you consistently spend a certain period of time on social media but want to make time to read, you can slowly incorporate readin into that time frame by starting off with one or two days a week and then building off of that base to 4-5 times a week and so on. Being able to understand what kind of behaviours incorporate the principles of neuroplasticity can allow you to approach it in a way that allows a more effective way of conditioning that change. Hence why it is quite important to understand the border line principles of neuroplasticity

Growth Mindset:

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant?

The growth Mindsetis a change of perspective of how you view things in life. It is taking the perspective of failure and altering it into a self-development tool instead of the typical tool of de grading and negativity towards yourself. For Example, In school if you failed a test you would bombarded with comments of how you failed and are incompetent because of this, this is one of the negative things about having a fixed mindset but if we were to view these in the perspective of a growth mindset. Failing a test isnt a setback its an oppurturnity to broaden your knowledge and dive into understanding what was done wrong and improving your skills and knowledge. This is why the Growth mindset is so important especially in education, it allows us to challenge our knowledge of things and expand upon that and to peak curiousity and gives the ability to walk into new situations without the anxiety and fear of failure as it an oppurturnity to develop yourself more.
