What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is essentially having a good understanding of emotions that occur in the standard day to day and in high stress enviroments and also identifying what those emotions are wether that be anger or joy. It is being able to grasp emotions being brought up and understanding how to not suppress them but manage them within reason of the circumstance, For Example, If someone had made a very rude comment to you about your appearance or something, having emotional Intelligence is understanding that the best thing in this situation is to walk away from that rather than start a fist fight. That is probably one of the most simplified examples but emotional intelligence has many complex layers to it for every specific situation which makes it a very complex technique to navigate without practice.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Just like stated in the previous question EQ is defined by the way you can identify your emotions and manage them in day to day situations and high intensity or high stress situations and to be able to manage them appropriately to the situation and to be able to manage your emotions around someone elses to, For example, If someone get you this nice gift for you but you in turn dont really like it or want it rather than being confrontational you can choose to appreciate the thought behind the gift because that what is matters like the saying "its the thought that counts". Although some people will vary with the level of EQ there are many ways to understand and further develop your EQ through discipline and perseverance.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is extremely important because it shows the levels of Emotional Intelligence we have which also corrulates to how we might fit better at a management position or in a high stress situation or even as simply to how you can discuss a extremely touchy subject with your friend like a breakup because those kinds of conversations can be extremely hard to navigate without upsetting the other party.
